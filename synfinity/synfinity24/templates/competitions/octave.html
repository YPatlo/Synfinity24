Octave

Compose, Create, Conquer: Where Originality Hits the Right Note!

Rules

Basic Rules and Regulations:

  1. Each school can send one team of 4 participants.
  2. Participants may bring any number of instruments or accompaniments (no karaoke). All instruments must be registered at the registration desk.
  3. Only original songs are permitted; any group found copying will be disqualified.
  4. Lyrics can be in English or any regional language, but only one language can be used throughout the song.
  5. Derogatory or inappropriate language is not allowed.
  6. Off-topic songs may be disqualified at the judges' discretion.
  7. No extra time will be provided under any circumstances.
  8. No devices are allowed during the event; they must be handed in at the registration desk beforehand.
  9. No retakes or restarts are allowed unless approved by the judges.
  10. The judges' decisions are final in all matters.
  11. Songs will be judged on melody, tune, lyrical meaning, synchronization of lyrics and music, coordination between instruments and vocals, presentation, time management, team involvement, and adherence to the event rules.

Phase 1 – The Qualifier:

  • This phase will take place before the event at the participants' respective schools.
  • Teams will receive 10 or more tunes and must create a 3-minute song using one or more of these tunes.
  • No external tunes can be added or modified.
  • The tracks will be sent to all schools at the same time, with the same deadline for submission.
  • The song must be a maximum of 3 minutes.
  • A video recording must be submitted to the host school before or on the deadline date.
  • No special effects, editing, or external support is allowed, or the team will be disqualified.
  • The top 8 teams, known as "the octave," will be selected from this round.
  • Basic rules and regulations apply.

Phase 2 – The Semifinal:

  • This phase will take place on the day of the event.
  • A common topic will be given to the 8 qualifying teams.
  • Teams will have 30 minutes to create a song based on the topic.
  • The song must be a maximum of 3 minutes.
  • Any genre is allowed, but the topic cannot be altered.
  • Teams will perform their songs live in front of the judges.
  • The top 3 teams will advance to the final round.
  • Basic rules and regulations apply.

Phase 3 – The Finale:

  • Teams will be given a short story, and must create a song in any genre that captures the essence or meaning of the story.
  • Teams may modify events or add new ones within the song.
  • Teams will have 5-10 minutes to read the story and plan their song.
  • 45 minutes will be given to compose the song.
  • Each team will have 5 minutes to perform their song.
  • Judges will decide the 1st, 2nd, and 3rd place winners based on this phase.
  • Basic rules and regulations apply.
